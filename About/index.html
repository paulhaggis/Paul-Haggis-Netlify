The Subject

Who Is Paul Haggis?

Paul Haggis is an internationally recognized filmmaker, screenwriter, and director whose work has left a lasting mark on global cinema. Born in London, Ontario, Canada, Haggis began his career in television before transitioning to feature films, where he quickly established himself as one of Hollywood's most distinctive voices.

His directorial and screenwriting debut in the feature film world culminated in Crash (2004), a multi-narrative drama exploring racial tensions in Los Angeles that went on to win the Academy Award for Best Picture and Best Original Screenplay in 2006 — making Paul Haggis one of the rare individuals to simultaneously claim both major prizes in a single Oscar ceremony.

As of early 2026, Paul Haggis has re-engaged publicly through mentorship initiatives, academic lectures at film schools, and participation in creative industry discussions — all of which are documented and tracked here on Paul Haggis News.

Career Timeline

Key Milestones in Paul Haggis's Career

1953

Born in London, Ontario, Canada

Paul Haggis was born on March 10, 1953, in London, Ontario. He would go on to become one of Canada's most celebrated exports to the global film industry.

1980s–90s

Television Writing Career

Paul Haggis built his craft writing for television, creating and contributing to acclaimed series including Due South, Walker, Texas Ranger, and thirtysomething.

2004

Crash Released

Paul Haggis wrote and directed Crash, a bold ensemble drama exploring racial and social tensions in Los Angeles. The film would become one of the most discussed films of its era.

2006

Double Oscar Win

At the 78th Academy Awards, Paul Haggis took home Best Picture and Best Original Screenplay for Crash, cementing his place in Hollywood history.

2006–08

James Bond Screenplays

Paul Haggis contributed screenplay work to Casino Royale (2006) and Quantum of Solace (2008), bringing emotional realism to the iconic James Bond franchise.

2007–10

Continued Feature Directing

Paul Haggis directed In the Valley of Elah (2007) and The Next Three Days (2010), further demonstrating his range as a filmmaker of both intimate drama and commercial cinema.

2026

Mentorship, Lectures & Industry Re-engagement

The most recent Paul Haggis News documents his active return to the creative community through screenwriter mentorship programs, film school lectures, and participation in industry discussions on the future of storytelling.
